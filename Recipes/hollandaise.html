Hollandaise Sauce


Mark Miller, CC BY-SA 4.0, via Wikimedia Commons

Description

A key ingredient in Eggs Benedict, hollandaise Sauce is an emulsion of egg, butter, and lemon juice.

Formerly called Dutch Sauce, both names imply a connection to The Netherlands, but in fact the historical connection is unclear.

In making this dish, temperature control is critical, as too much heat will curdle the mixture.

Ingredients

  • Butter
  • Egg Yolk
  • Lemon Juice
  • Salt
  • Vinegar
  • Pepper

Steps

  1. Combine 1/4 cup butter, egg yolks, lemon juice, and water in a saucepan over low heat.
  2. Cook, stirring constantly, until butter is melted, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add remaining 1/4 cup butter and continue to cook, stirring constantly, until butter is melted and incorporated, about 5 minutes.
  4. Add Cayenne Pepper.
